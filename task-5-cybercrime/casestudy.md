### UPI/Online Payment Fraud :-

What it is :-  
In UPI/Online Payment Fraud people are tricked into paying money to random people,account or organization , or by tricking people into revealing sensitive banking information like bank pin.

How it typically happens :-     
It happens through many ways like :-
1) a) Unknown person pretending to be your freind first  
   b) and then asking money by giving reasons like medical emergencies  
   c) Blocking you,not responding etc once the payment is done  
2) a) Fraud person pretending to be goverment or bank member first  
   b) Asking you your personal details like bank pin in name of check or safety  
   c) Then using this information to do fraud  
3) a) By sending links containg virus  
   b) which enetrs your system and steals all important information once you click on it  

Who is usually targeted :-  
Common targets include students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems. Small business owners and online sellers are also frequently targeted.

what the consequences are :-  
Consequences can be serious like loss of huge amount or total money, mental stress and torture , loss of trust on others and digital system , a lot of waste of time efforts trying to recover money that to not gauranteed that money will come back.

Realistic fictional scenario :-  
Rohan, a first-year college student, had recently started handling his own expenses through UPI apps. One evening, he got a WhatsApp message from an unknown number, but the profile picture looked exactly like his school friend Arjun. The message was urgent: “Bro, I’m in the hospital, I need ₹5,000 immediately.”

Without thinking too much, Rohan replied. The person quickly explained that it was a medical emergency and sent a QR code, asking him to transfer the money as soon as possible. Rohan felt a bit unsure for a moment, but the situation seemed serious, so he decided to help.

He scanned the QR code and entered his UPI PIN, assuming the money would go directly to his friend. Within seconds, the transaction was completed.

Later, just to check, Rohan called Arjun on his saved number. To his surprise, Arjun had no idea about any message or emergency. That’s when Rohan realized something was wrong—he had been scammed.

The incident left him shocked and frustrated. It made him understand how easily someone can misuse trust online. After this, Rohan became much more careful. He started verifying such requests before sending money and warned his friends to stay alert as well.
